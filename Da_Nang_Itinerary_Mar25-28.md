## Day 1 — Wednesday, March 25: Arrival & First Taste of Da Nang

### Afternoon: Settle In & Hit the Beach
- Arrive at Da Nang International Airport (DAD). Grab a **Grab taxi** to your hotel — rides to the beach area run about 80,000–120,000 VND ($3–5).
- Drop your bags and head to **My Khe Beach**. March weather is warm (25–29°C) with low rain, so conditions should be great for a swim or a stroll along the paved coastal path.

### Evening: Street Food Crawl & Dragon Bridge
- Walk to **Bánh Xèo Bà Dưỡng** (K280/23 Hoàng Diệu) for the city's famous crispy sizzling crepes (*bánh xèo*) and grilled pork skewers (*nem lụi*). Budget ~60,000–80,000 VND for a full meal.
- Stroll along the **Hàn River** toward the **Dragon Bridge**. On weekends it breathes fire and water at 9 PM, but even on a Wednesday evening the bridge is beautifully lit and the riverside is lively.
- Stop at **Son Tra Night Market** if you're still hungry — great BBQ stalls and grilled squid.

### Practical Tips
- Pick up a local SIM at the airport (Viettel or Mobifone, ~100,000 VND for plenty of data).
- Grab is your best friend for transport — cheap, reliable, and no language barrier needed.

## Day 2 — Thursday, March 26: Ba Na Hills & Golden Bridge

### Morning: Early Start
- **Depart by 7:00 AM** from your hotel to beat the crowds. Book a Grab car (~250,000–300,000 VND / $10–12 one-way) or arrange a return shuttle through your hotel. The drive takes about 45 minutes.
- **Tickets:** ~750,000 VND (~$30) for adults, which includes the cable car and all park attractions. Buy online at [booking.sunworld.vn](https://booking.sunworld.vn/) to skip the queue.

### At the Park (8 AM – 3 PM)
- **Golden Bridge** — The iconic giant stone hands. Arrive early for photos without the tour group crowds.
- **French Village** — Wander the European-style streets, grab a coffee, and enjoy the cooler mountain air (it's noticeably cooler up top, bring a light layer).
- **Fantasy Park** — Indoor amusement park with rides and games, included in your ticket.
- **Lunch** — The on-site buffet restaurants are decent but pricey (~200,000–300,000 VND). Alternatively, grab lighter bites at the food stalls throughout the park.

### Evening: Check In & Relax
- Head back to Da Nang and check into the **Mercure**.
- For dinner, try **Bé Anh Seafood** — a communal-table spot where you pick your own fresh seafood. Solo-friendly and lively. Budget around 200,000–400,000 VND depending on what you order.

## Day 3 — Friday, March 27: Hoi An Ancient Town

### Morning: Head to Hoi An
- **Depart 7:00 AM** from Mercure Ba Na Hills — check out, take the cable car down (~30 minutes), then grab a Grab taxi to Hoi An (~1 hour from the base). You'll arrive around 9:00–9:30 AM.
- Pick up an **Old Town ticket** (120,000 VND) which gives you entry to 5 heritage sites including the Japanese Covered Bridge, assembly halls, and old merchant houses.

### Daytime: Explore the Ancient Town
- Wander the **yellow-walled streets** of the UNESCO World Heritage old quarter. Highlights include the Fujian Assembly Hall, Tan Ky Old House, and the Central Market.
- **Lunch at the Central Market** — Try *cao lầu* (Hoi An's signature thick noodle dish) and *bánh mì* from the market stalls. Hoi An is widely considered to have the best bánh mì in Vietnam.
- Optional: Book a **cooking class** (many half-day options from ~$25–35 that include a market tour) or a **basket boat ride** through the coconut palm forest at Cam Thanh.

### Evening: Lanterns & Night Market
- Stay into the evening — Hoi An transforms after dark. The **lantern-lit streets** along the Thu Bồn River are magical.
- Browse the **Hoi An Night Market** for souvenirs, custom clothing (Hoi An is famous for its tailors), and street snacks.
- Grab a taxi back to Da Nang afterward, or book a return shuttle. The ride back at night is quick and cheap.

## Day 4 — Saturday, March 28: Marble Mountains & Departure

### Morning: Marble Mountains (7:15–9:00 AM)
- Check out of Seashore Hotel & Apartment and head to **Marble Mountains** (Ngũ Hành Sơn), about 15 minutes by Grab (~50,000 VND).
- **Entrance:** 40,000 VND + 15,000 VND for the elevator (recommended going up, then take the stairs down).
- Enter via **Gate 2** — easier climb and closer to the elevator.
- Must-see: **Huyền Không Cave** — a massive cavern with shafts of sunlight piercing through the collapsed ceiling. Stunning for photos.
- Allow 1.5 hours. **Leave by 9:00 AM sharp** — your flight departs at 10:45.

### Departure (9:00 AM)
- Head straight to the airport from Marble Mountains (~15 minutes). Arriving by 9:15 gives you 90 minutes before the 10:45 departure — enough buffer for check-in and security.

## Packing Notes for March in Da Nang

- Light, breathable clothing (it's warm and humid)
- A light jacket or hoodie for Ba Na Hills (cooler at altitude) and air-conditioned spaces
- Comfortable walking shoes for Marble Mountains and Hoi An cobblestones
- Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat
- Swimwear for My Khe Beach
- A small daypack for daily essentials
